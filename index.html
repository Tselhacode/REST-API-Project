SOOK CHING MASSACRE

Japanese-occupied Singapore

18 February - 4 March 1942

What is Sook Ching?

"Sook Ching" is the transliteration of the Chinese words 肃清. It means "to purge through cleansing" the anti-Japanese elements in the local Chinese community.A mass screening was conducted and an estimated 40000 to 50000 chinese males aged between 18 and 50 were killed.

Mass Screening

Screenings centres all over Singapore eliminated anyone suspected to be "anti-japanese". The standards of screening were arbitrary and depended on the subjective judgement of the interrogators. Those who passed the screening bore a square ink mark stamped "examined" and those who failed bore a triangular mark and send to the killing sites.
